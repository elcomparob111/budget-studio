### Why this matters

App Store review, trademark filings, and acquisition diligence often ask: who owns the logo, and can you commercially exploit it? AI-generated marks with missing tool/license records create **Medium** IP risk (see [`../LEGAL_SWEEP.md`](../LEGAL_SWEEP.md) and [`../docs/PRODUCTION_AUDIT.md`](../docs/PRODUCTION_AUDIT.md)).

### Required actions (owner)

1. Identify which tool generated the art (e.g. Midjourney, DALL·E, Ideogram, Cursor image, etc.).
2. Archive: generation date, account, prompt, and a screenshot/PDF of that tool’s commercial terms then in force.
3. Confirm family dog photos are owned by Roberto Munoz (or obtain written permission from co-owners).
4. Confirm no third-party stock, celebrity likeness, or trademarked character was used as a reference.
5. Either:
   - **Clear:** attach evidence to this file (paths or private vault reference), or
   - **Replace:** commission original art or regenerate under a tool with clear commercial rights; update icons; keep old files out of marketing until retired.
6. Optional: register copyright in the final mark after clearance; consider trademark for the word mark separately ([`TRADEMARK.md`](TRADEMARK.md)).

### Interim policy

- OK for family / private beta use.
- Before paid App Store listing or public commercial marketing: complete steps 1–5.
- Do not claim “original hand-drawn illustration” in marketing until provenance is clear.
